About Dental Crowns

Restore. Protect. Beautify.

A dental crown (or cap) is a tooth-shaped covering that encases the entire visible portion of a damaged tooth. Crowns restore a tooth's shape, size, strength, and appearance when decay, fractures, or after root canal treatment compromises its integrity.

When Do You Need a Crown?

  • After root canal treatment to protect the tooth
  • Severely decayed tooth that can't be filled
  • Cracked or fractured tooth at risk of breaking
  • Replacing a large, failing filling
  • Covering a dental implant
  • Cosmetic enhancement of misshapen tooth

Crown Types We Offer

Zirconia Crowns

Strongest material — ideal for back teeth. Looks completely natural. Most popular choice.

Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal (PFM)

Metal inner core for strength, porcelain exterior for aesthetics. Cost-effective option.

All-Ceramic / E-Max

Best aesthetics for front teeth. Ultra-translucent material matches natural tooth light.

Metal Crowns

Gold or silver alloy for extreme durability. Used for molars in grinding patients.

The Process

How We Fit Your Crown

1

Examination & Planning

X-rays to assess tooth structure, select appropriate crown type, shade matching.

2

Tooth Preparation

Tooth trimmed under local anaesthesia, impressions taken for lab fabrication.

3

Temporary Crown

Temporary crown placed while permanent one is fabricated in 5–7 days.

4

Permanent Fitting

Crown bonded with dental cement, bite checked and adjusted for perfect fit.
