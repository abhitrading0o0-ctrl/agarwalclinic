What Are Dental Implants?

The Gold Standard in Tooth Replacement

A dental implant is a titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone, acting as an artificial tooth root. Over a healing period of 3–6 months, the implant fuses with the bone (osseointegration), creating a rock-solid foundation for a crown.

Unlike dentures or bridges, implants are permanent, don't require modification of adjacent teeth, and maintain jawbone density — preventing the facial structure changes that come with tooth loss.

  • Looks, feels, and functions like a real tooth
  • 60-70% stronger bite force compared to dentures
  • Prevents bone loss and facial structure collapse
  • No dietary restrictions — eat anything you love
  • Lifetime solution with proper care
  • Zero impact on adjacent healthy teeth

The Process

How Dental Implants Work

A proven, step-by-step procedure designed for safety and long-term success.

1

Consultation & Scan

Digital X-ray and 3D CBCT scan to assess bone density and plan the implant precisely.

2

Implant Placement

Titanium post placed under local anaesthesia in a 30-45 minute procedure. Virtually painless.

3

Osseointegration

The implant fuses with your jawbone over 3–6 months, creating an unshakeable foundation.

4

Crown Fitting

A custom-crafted, tooth-coloured crown is attached. Your new permanent tooth is complete.
