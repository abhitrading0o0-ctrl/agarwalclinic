About Root Canal

Save Your Tooth. End the Pain.

When decay or infection reaches the inner pulp of a tooth, a root canal is the only way to save it without extraction. Modern rotary endodontics has made this procedure fast, comfortable, and highly predictable.

Dr. Ajay Agarwal uses apex locators and nickel-titanium rotary files that clean and shape the root canals with precision — in significantly less time than traditional methods.

Signs You May Need Root Canal:

  • Severe toothache, especially when biting
  • Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold
  • Darkening or discolouration of the tooth
  • Swollen, tender gums near the tooth
  • A persistent pimple or abscess on the gums
  • Cracked or chipped tooth with deep decay

The Procedure

Root Canal Step by Step

1

Diagnosis & X-Ray

Digital X-ray to assess infection extent and confirm root canal is needed.

2

Anaesthesia

Local anaesthetic administered. You'll feel pressure but no pain during the procedure.

3

Canal Cleaning

Infected pulp removed, canals cleaned, shaped, and disinfected with rotary files.

4

Sealing & Crown

Canals sealed with gutta-percha, tooth protected with a final crown for strength.
